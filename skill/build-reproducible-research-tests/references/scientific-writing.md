# Scientific writing

Apply this reference to reports, paper sections, result narratives, figure discussions, abstracts, and other reader-facing scientific prose. README and reproduction instructions are technical documents and follow a different disclosure policy.

## Evidence before prose

- Build a claim-evidence ledger before drafting. For each intended claim, record the supporting result table, verification record, inspected source, or accepted external citation.
- If support is absent, ask the user for evidence or remove the claim.
- Distinguish measured facts, derived results, interpretation, hypothesis, recommendation, and unknowns.
- Do not turn correlation into causation or descriptive differences into significance without a valid analysis.
- Preserve negative and null results when they are supported.

## Formulas

When mathematics is involved, include the formula rather than only naming the method. Put every standalone formula on its own line. On the immediately following line, write `其中，……` and define every letter and symbol. Do not indent that explanation as a new paragraph.

State units, weighting, averaging domain, denominator conventions, and zero-denominator handling when they affect interpretation.

## Chinese typography

In Chinese prose, do not automatically insert spaces around English words, numbers, abbreviations, model names, software names, variable names, units, or proper nouns. Write forms such as `DeepONet神经网络`、`Fluent计算结果`、`CFD数据集`、`1.282K`.

Preserve spaces required by formulas, code, commands, English sentences, citations, or formal formats.

## Reader-facing independence

Write for the declared target reader, who may know nothing about the conversation or work process. Introduce the scientific background, method, evidence, and conclusion naturally.

Do not expose:

- user instructions or conversation history;
- revision, deletion, or negotiation history;
- absolute paths, internal directory names, script names, code locations, or backstage reference materials;
- phrases such as “根据你的要求”“按照修改意见”“结合前面讨论”“修改后如下” or similar process narration.

Internal files may support understanding and verification; translate their evidence into scientific content rather than naming the files.

## Direct scientific style

- Prefer direct, affirmative statements supported by evidence.
- Avoid defensive constructions that pre-empt misunderstandings that have not arisen.
- Limit weak modifiers such as “可能”“或许”“一定程度上”“某种意义上”“有助于” to cases where uncertainty is quantified or scientifically necessary.
- Avoid unnecessary disclaimers about the author's capability or generic future work.
- Do not mechanically enumerate paragraphs with repeated “首先、其次、最后” or “第一、第二、第三”. Use paragraph order, causal progression, repeated keywords, and semicolon parallelism to express structure.
- Define specialized terms at the reader's expected level and keep notation consistent.

## Final self-check

Confirm that:

- every substantive claim has evidence;
- formulas and symbols are fully defined;
- numbers, units, signs, significant digits, and uncertainty match verified results;
- the document stands alone for the target reader;
- no backstage information or internal path leaked into the prose;
- wording is direct rather than defensive;
- structure reads naturally rather than as a generic template;
- tables, figures, captions, and prose are mutually consistent.
